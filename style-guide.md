## Brand Overview

### Mission
Carbon Upscale empowers individuals and organizations to measure, reduce, and offset their carbon footprint through accessible tools and actionable insights. We are committed to fighting climate change by making sustainable choices easier to understand and implement.

### Brand Promise
We simplify sustainability by providing clear, data-driven tools that translate complex environmental impact into understandable metrics and achievable goals.

### Target Audience
- Environmentally conscious individuals seeking to understand and reduce their personal impact
- Organizations looking to measure and improve their sustainability performance
- Climate scientists and sustainability professionals

## Logo

### Primary Logo
The Carbon Upscale logo is a distinctive fingerprint-like design that symbolizes both individual identity and personal responsibility for environmental impact. The overlapping lines create a circular pattern, representing the cyclical nature of carbon measurement, reduction, and offsetting.

### Logo Variations
1. **Standard Logo** - Black fingerprint design for use on light backgrounds
2. **Reversed Logo** - White fingerprint design for use on dark or colored backgrounds
3. **Combination Mark** - Logo with "Carbon Upscale" wordmark

### Clear Space
Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "C" in "Carbon." This space should be free from other graphic elements, text, or the edge of a printed page.

### Minimum Size
To ensure legibility, the logo should never appear smaller than 24px in height for digital applications or 0.5 inches in print.

### Improper Logo Usage
- Do not stretch or distort the logo
- Do not rotate the logo
- Do not change the logo colors
- Do not place the logo on visually busy backgrounds
- Do not add effects such as shadows or outlines

## Typography

### Primary Typefaces

#### Fraunces (Serif)
Used for headings, display text, and important statements.

**Weights:**
- Bold (700) - Primary headlines
- Regular (400) - Secondary headlines

**Usage Examples:**
- Main page headlines
- Section titles
- Quote attributions

#### Inter (Sans-Serif)
Used for body copy, paragraphs, and general content.

**Weights:**
- Regular (400) - Body text
- Medium (500) - Emphasized text
- Light (300) - Supporting text

**Usage Examples:**
- Paragraphs
- Descriptions
- Data labels

#### Manrope (Sans-Serif)
Used specifically for navigation, buttons, and UI elements.

**Weights:**
- Bold (700) - Primary buttons, navigation
- Medium (500) - Secondary buttons, interactive elements
- Regular (400) - Form labels, captions

**Usage Examples:**
- Navigation links
- Button text
- Form elements

## Visual Elements

### Data Visualization
Carbon Upscale uses clean, accessible data visualizations to communicate carbon footprint metrics. Bar charts, line graphs, and area charts should follow these guidelines:

- Use the primary color palette for data points
- Include clear, concise labels
- Provide context for the data being presented
- Ensure accessible color contrast for all visualization elements

### Icons
Icons should be simple, consistent, and meaningful. Use outlined icons with a 2px stroke weight for UI elements and filled icons for emphasis or in smaller contexts.

Icon style should be geometric and minimal, avoiding unnecessary details while maintaining clarity of purpose.

### Illustrations
When custom illustrations are used, they should be:
- Simple and geometric in style
- Aligned with the brand color palette
- Focused on explaining complex sustainability concepts
- Consistent in style across all applications

## Voice and Tone

### Brand Voice Attributes
- **Knowledgeable**: We share expertise without being condescending
- **Empowering**: We motivate rather than shame
- **Accessible**: We translate complex environmental concepts into understandable terms
- **Solution-focused**: We emphasize practical actions that make a difference

### Writing Style
- Use active voice and direct address
- Keep sentences clear and concise
- Avoid jargon without context or explanation
- Balance scientific accuracy with accessibility
- Lead with benefits and practical applications


## Applications

### Documentation
Documentation materials should:
- Use consistent headers and formatting
- Include branded elements while prioritizing readability
- Maintain a structured information hierarchy
- Follow the established color palette for highlighting and organization

### Social Media
Social media content should:
- Use approved brand colors and typography when creating custom graphics
- Maintain the established voice and tone
- Include the logo where appropriate, following clear space guidelines
